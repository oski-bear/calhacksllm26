Sources:
- Main site: https://ai.hackberkeley.org/

## Overview

- Event: UC Berkeley AI Hackathon 2026.
- Organizer: Hackathons @ Berkeley / Cal Hacks team.
- Dates: Saturday, June 20 to Sunday, June 21, 2026.
- Venue: Martin Luther King Jr. Student Union, 2495 Bancroft Way, Berkeley, CA 94720.
- Format: fully in-person.
- Theme: build with large language models, open source APIs, and modern AI tools.
- Main site describes the event as 24 hours with 1300+ hackers and 300+ projects.
- Main site marketing says "$100,000 in prizes"; Devpost/live currently list $25,800+ in prizes plus other prizes. For prize targeting, use Devpost/live details because they enumerate prize categories.

## Submission Requirements

From Devpost/live site:

- Submit by Sunday, June 21 at 11:00 AM PDT.
- You can continue hacking and editing submission details until 12:00 PM, but the project must be created/submitted by 11:00 AM.
- Include a 2-3 sentence elevator pitch/summary.
- Include a detailed project description with images and links.
- Include team location/table number.
- Include team name.
- Include an image of the project.
- Include a GitHub repository for any code to be considered.
- Judges will evaluate both the Devpost submission and in-person judging.
- Presentations must be 5 minutes or under.

## Team and Eligibility Notes

- Main site FAQ says teams can have up to 4 people.
- Live site FAQ says teams can be 1-4 people.
- Devpost overview says team required: 2 to 4 members. Since this team has 3 people, you are safely within all stated ranges.
- Main site says participants must be undergraduate/graduate students or recent grads from Fall 2024 or later, age 18+.
- Devpost says all team members must be accepted and confirmed hackers.

## Rules That Matter

- Ideation before hacking starts is allowed.
- All implementation must occur during the official hacking period.
- Previous projects are not allowed.
- Submitting prior code/project work can lead to disqualification.
- All hackers must be physically present for judging.
- Join and monitor Slack; live site says participants are required to be on Slack for updates.

## Judging Criteria

2026 Devpost criteria:

- Application: feasible real-life use, actual benefit to users.
- Functionality/Quality: free of major bugs, appealing enough UI/UX, understandable project.
- Creativity: unique, innovative, new solution or better way of doing something.
- Technical Complexity: team shows solid knowledge of the technologies used.

Live site criteria:

- Impact.
- Functionality.
- Technical complexity.
- Creativity.

## Tracks

Ddoski's World:

- Social impact.
- Education access, civic platforms, environmental impact, systemic inequity, political participation, community organizing, real-world social challenges.

Ddoski's Toolbox:

- Tools for developers, creators, and knowledge workers.
- Developer utilities, CLIs, APIs, automation scripts, workflow apps, project management, writing/design aids.
- The site says usefulness, usability, and execution matter, not just the idea.

Ddoski's Lab:

- Science and engineering.
- Health tech, medical tools, hardware prototypes, embedded systems, biotech applications, research tools, complex engineering problems.
- Software, hardware, or both.

Ddoski's Playground:

- Catch-all for creative/unconventional projects.
- Games, interactive experiences, generative art, humor-driven hacks, experimental interfaces.
- Evaluated on originality, execution, and how compelling the experience is.
